📊 Level 3 · Starting Conversations

But how can I know if my campaign is working?

A creator-friendly guide to measure whether your climate content is truly making impact — without needing a data team.

7 steps · fully DIY · zero painful spreadsheets

So you're posting about climate, renewable energy, just transition — or you want to — and now you're wondering:

"Okay but… is it working?"

Great question. And I promise the answer does not require a statistics degree, a team of analysts, or a dashboard that looks like a spaceship.

1
Step 1
Know Your North Star
›

Before numbers, before analytics — you need one simple compass.

Your goal is to help normalize climate and renewable energy conversations in your niche.

Not to become a climate expert. Not to lecture. Not to turn your feed into a science museum. Just to include the energy transition into the world you already create — beauty, books, food, fashion, gaming, home, lifestyle, whatever your lane is.

Your impact = more people in your community talking about renewable energy in ways that feel intuitive, fun, or relevant to real life.

2
Step 2
Build Your Tiny Logframe
›

A "logical framework" sounds like something on a government whiteboard — but you can shrink it into something creator-sized. Just answer three questions:

A. Big change you want to support
  • More public support for renewable energy
  • A culture where clean energy feels normal
  • More diverse climate stories in everyday niches
B. What you can realistically influence (outcomes)
  • More energy conversations in your comments
  • Followers DMing questions or tagging friends
  • KOL peers resharing your climate posts
  • More people at your events or livestreams
C. What you'll actually do (outputs)
  • Online: Reels, TikToks, carousels, stories, educational content, humor, storytelling
  • Community: IG Live, offline meetups, book clubs, collabs, pop-ups

That's it. You now have a logframe — creator edition.

3
Step 3
Create Your Own Baseline
›

Look at your last 10 non-climate posts:

  • What's the average reach?
  • What's the average engagement rate?
  • What kind of comments do you usually get?

Write it down anywhere — Notes app, Notion, back of a receipt. This is your personal benchmark.

Did the climate post perform above, below, or differently from my usual content? That difference is data. And more importantly — it's direction.
4
Step 4
Track These 6 Indicators
›

You don't have a data team — but you have the most important metrics right inside your app.

01
Reach
Are more people seeing your climate content than expected?
02
Engagement
Likes, saves, shares — but especially: are people responding with curiosity or excitement?
03
Sentiment
Are comments supportive, curious, inspired, playful? This is where the real magic happens.
04
Community Conversations
Did people tag friends, start their own posts, DM questions, use your hashtag? → That's cultural impact.
05
KOL Echo
Did other creators repost, reference your message, or start similar conversations? This is how narratives spread.
06
Offline Ripples
How many came to your events? Did they post? DM you? This counts just as much as numbers.
5
Step 5
Use the "3 Post Rule"
›

Don't judge impact from one post. Try at least three — then compare with your baseline.

✨
Post 1
Fun or storytelling post
🧠
Post 2
Informative or myth-busting post
💬
Post 3
Community activation — invite, poll, question box

You'll start seeing patterns: "Storytelling performs best." "My audience loves practical tips." "Humor works better than big-picture concepts." This information is gold.

6
Step 6
Choose Your Own Hashtag
›

Think of a hashtag as your little lighthouse — a tiny marker that gathers all the posts, ripples, and side-conversations your content inspires. Join our regional conversation or birth your own:

#thegoodchatasia #EnergyIsEveryday #CleanEnergyAtHome #PoweringMyLife #MyJustTransition

Every time someone uses it, you can see how far your influence has wandered and which corners of the internet have started talking about renewable energy because of you.

7
Step 7
Ask One Simple Question
›
Your golden question
"Did this spark any new conversation in my niche that wasn't there before?"

After every climate post, look for tiny signs:

Did someone ask a question they've never asked before?
Did a follower DM: "I never thought about it like this"?
Did someone tag a friend or use your hashtag?
Did a small pocket of your community start a conversation you didn't have to lead?
Did your comment section sound different from your usual content?

If the answer to any of these is yes — congratulations, your campaign is working.

Impact doesn't always look like fireworks. Sometimes it looks like three thoughtful comments, one brave question, or a quiet DM from someone who learned something new.

Culture doesn't shift because something goes viral. Culture shifts because something becomes familiar — showing up again and again in ways that feel natural, personal, and part of everyday life. That's what you're building. One post, one conversation, one ripple at a time.

TL;DR — The Creator-Friendly Impact Checklist
If you can answer "yes" to any of these, you are making real impact. Tap to check off what applies.
My climate posts perform at or above my usual baseline
My audience comments more thoughtfully on these posts
People DM me questions or stories
Other creators have reshared or referenced my content
Someone in my community used my hashtag or made a related post
My niche now has at least one renewable-energy conversation it didn't have before
Check any box above — that's mainstreaming. That's culture shift. That's impact.
