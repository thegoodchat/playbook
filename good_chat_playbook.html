About this playbook

🌏How to use this playbook
▼

Think of this space like a neighborhood library where no one shushes you: wander in, pull random books off the shelf, flip through whatever sparks joy, leave the rest exactly where they are, or slip in a note for the next person who comes by.

Everything here is meant to be skimmed, borrowed, annotated, remixed, and taken home in your metaphorical tote bag.

There's no 'correct' route through the shelves. No Dewey Decimal destiny you must fulfill. Just follow what feels useful to you, and to the communities you're building with.

🤷‍♀️New to Notion?
▼

No worries — Notion looks fancy but behaves like a very chill digital notebook.

  • Click anywhere to read.
  • Use the left sidebar to jump between pages.
  • The triangle toggle bullets open and close like tiny accordion secrets.
  • Nothing here will bite.

You'll have comment access — highlight text and leave thoughts, questions, ideas, or spicy takes.

Play around. Touch the buttons. Nothing will explode.

🔓Open-source, because gatekeeping is boring
▼

Everything here is free, accessible, and made to be passed around. Screenshots welcome. Copy-paste encouraged.

If it helps someone spark a conversation, run a workshop, or start a tiny climate uprising with their cousins, perfect.

🥥Rooted in Southeast Asia
▼

This toolkit was born out of real conversations, real joy, and real work in the Philippines, Thailand, and Indonesia.

It speaks our languages, our humor, our chaos, our crises, and our ways of taking care of each other. It's not 'global'. It's glocal, with sweaty humidity and traffic jams baked in.

🪴FAQs
▼

Can I copy parts of this toolkit?

Yes, please. Copy, paste, remix — whatever helps you and your community.

Is this really open source?

Absolutely. Use it, share it, pass it along. This playbook survives through circulation, not secrecy.

I'm based outside Southeast Asia. Is this still for me?

Totally. Just know this was built from lived experiences in the Philippines, Thailand, and Indonesia. Adapt as needed.

Can I contribute?

Yes! Drop ideas, questions, or suggestions in the margins. You can also email us.

What if I'm overwhelmed?

Same. Take one page at a time. Or close the tab, drink water, breathe, and return when your neurons feel less toasted.
