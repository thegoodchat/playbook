📽️ Resource 1 · Film
The Week
A three-part experience by Frédéric Laloux & Hélène Gérin

Across its three episodes, The Week helps you understand the unfolding environmental breakdown and its direct impacts on people's lives, health, and finances.

We encourage you to watch it in a small group — invite friends, colleagues, or family — and watch one episode per day. It's a powerful way to process your emotions around the climate crisis and begin meaningful conversations together.

Can't do it in a group? No problem — it's a documentary in 3 episodes and works just as well solo.

🃏 Resource 2 · Workshop
Climate Fresk
A collaborative, science-based workshop to understand climate change — together.

Created in France by Cédric Ringenbach in 2015, the Climate Fresk is based on the findings of the IPCC reports ↗.

The format is a 3-hour group activity where participants work together to map out 42 cards illustrating the links between human activity, greenhouse gas emissions, global warming, and its social, economic, and environmental impacts.

The approach is both educational and experiential — combining science, teamwork, and emotions to make the complexity of climate change accessible to everyone. 🌍
