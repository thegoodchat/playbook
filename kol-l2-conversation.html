The Power of Conversations: a New & Soft Form of Activism

Systems change when a new way of thinking becomes the norm.

Systems don't change just because a law gets passed. They change when enough people start thinking differently, imagining differently, acting differently. That's what we mean by large-scale mindset change. It's not about one epic speech at the UN — it's about thousands of tiny shifts in kitchens, group chats, livestream comments, and bus-stop gossip. That's where new futures get normalized.

🔑 In the masterclass below, you'll explore the power of conversation as a gentle yet effective form of activism — and gain practical tips and tactics to spark and shape meaningful dialogues that drive systemic change.

How Conversations Shift Social Norms

Think about it: fashion trends, slang, memes, even whole lifestyle choices — none of them started in policy papers. They started in conversations.

A girl posts about her thrifted outfit. Someone DMs you about plant-based recipes. A cousin teases you for carrying a reusable cup — and then buys one themselves a week later.

That's how social norms bend: not by telling people what's right, but by showing them what's normal. And normal is powerful.

When enough people treat climate care as obvious, decision-makers don't have to be convinced — they have to keep up.

🎙️

Why Storytelling, Discussion & Visibility Matter

You don't need technical expertise to be part of this. Honestly, most people tune out when you start dropping policy acronyms. What they lean in for are stories:

🌧️ A thread about how your city flooded and you biked through it with soggy sneakers.
👵 A TikTok on how your grandma composts without calling it composting.
🌬️ A casual chat on why your aircon broke and you realized fans + shade trees actually work.

Storytelling makes climate human. Discussion makes it communal. Visibility makes it undeniable.

Every conversation you have is a micro-rebellion against apathy.
Small and unpolished — but alive. And alive is exactly what we need.

What is the Tipping Point — and why does it matter?

The tipping point is the moment at which it takes more energy to resist a change than to participate in it. Research shows this threshold is lower than most people think.

25%
The tipping point threshold
According to research cited by Malcolm Gladwell, when just 25% of a group adopts a new norm, the change becomes self-sustaining. You don't need a majority. You need a committed, vocal quarter.
Example: Mental health conversations. Before COVID-19, it was taboo to talk about anxiety or therapy. After countless public and personal conversations during the pandemic, it became normal — and policy and business culture followed. That's a tipping point in action.
🤝

4 Principles of a Meaningful Conversation

Not all conversations have the power to change society positively. A meaningful conversation is one where both people leave having received something of value — a new insight, a feeling of being understood, or a sense of hope.

These four principles are the backbone of how Good Chat fellows approach dialogue.

1
Humility
›

Come in knowing that you don't have all the answers. Especially when talking about something as complex as the energy transition, we each hold just a small piece of the truth.

Humility keeps the door open for learning — and for connection.

2
Curiosity
›

Instead of trying to convince or lecture, get genuinely interested. Ask follow-up questions. Reflect back what you heard — things like, "It sounds like you're worried about the cost, is that right?"

That simple reflection makes people feel heard, and often helps them open up more deeply. Curiosity turns monologue into dialogue.

3
Generosity
›

Bring something to the table — a story, a resource, or even just kindness. You don't have to be an expert, but you can offer your experience as a gift.

Sometimes it's as small as saying: "I used to think that too, until I saw…" or sharing a personal example. Generosity disarms defensiveness.

4
Accountability
›

What we say matters. Be honest, be kind, and follow up. If you promise to share a link, share it. If someone raises a question you can't answer, circle back later.

That follow-through builds trust — and trust is what turns a nice chat into a lasting relationship.

When humility, curiosity, generosity, and accountability come together, a conversation moves beyond opinion-trading. It becomes a space where transformation can happen — for the listener, the speaker, and the community around them.
