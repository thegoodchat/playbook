🚀 Level 3 · Starting Conversations

How to launch a first campaign when I've never talked about climate change with my audience before?

The most powerful conversations can't be fully scripted — so your plan shouldn't be either.

⚠️ Before you start — two things to do first
1
Watch the "Power of Conversations" training — understand why dialogue is one of the most effective ways to influence attitudes and inspire collective action in SEA. This is the foundation of your campaign.
→ The Power of Conversations: a New & Soft Form of Activism
2
Create your "Story of Self, Us, and Now" — clarify your community driver and define why this moment matters for them.
→ Your Story, Your Why — Finding Your Place in the Movement
⚠️ Important mindset shift
This is not a traditional communication campaign.

We're not producing a series of polished posts, events, or slogans planned in advance. What we're building is a strategy — a structured plan to create and sustain conversations about the energy transition across multiple spaces and over time.

Your content and events plan will evolve with how the conversations are happening. The goal is not only to raise awareness, but to make people care enough to talk — with their families, friends, and communities.

Think of yourself not as a campaign manager, but as a conversation enabler.

Your objective is to design opportunities to discuss Renewable Energy — through online content, small gatherings, collaborations, or everyday exchanges — that allow people to engage naturally. The more organic, authentic, and relatable, the more powerful.

💭 What kinds of conversations have influenced me in my own life?
💭 How can I recreate those kinds of exchanges for others?

This is the foundation of your campaign: not communication for visibility, but conversation for change.

What is a campaign?

A campaign is a time-bound series of materials, content, and activities unified by one purpose, one central message, and a consistent visual and verbal identity.

📱
Social Media Campaign
A series of posts based on your content and message — published across your platforms over time.
🤝
In-Person Campaign
Gatherings you organize, lead, and facilitate — bringing conversations into physical spaces.

The 3 Key Elements of Any Campaign

Whether online or offline, every campaign is built on the same three foundations. Click each to explore.

👥
Element 1
Audience — who you're talking to
›

Goal: Understand your current community and how to reach them.

Reflect on:

  • Who is your current community? Age range, gender, interests, opportunities and challenges
  • What do they interact with you about? Topics, habits, behaviors — what already gets them talking?

The clearer your picture of your audience, the more naturally your campaign will connect with them.

💬
Element 2
Messaging — what you're saying
›

Goal: Decide what you want people to think, feel, and do.

Think / Feel / Do Framework
Think
What do you want your audience to understand about renewable energy?
Feel
What emotion do you want them to feel?
Do
What action do you want them to take?
Messaging House Tool
Key message
The main idea of your campaign
Values
What makes your message appealing or relevant to your audience?
Support
Proof, examples, or reasons people should believe your message
✍️
Element 3
Content — how you're saying it
›

Goal: Make renewable energy relatable using "entry points" your community already cares about.

Entry point examples:

Electricity rates Flooding & climate impacts Extreme weather Making homes less hot Understanding the power sector

For each entry point, brainstorm at least 3 content examples using storytelling and daily-life relevance. Start with what feels most natural — that's usually what resonates most with your audience too.

💡
Planning is important. Flexibility is essential.

Conversations are living things — they evolve, shift direction, and take us somewhere unexpected. Your plan should be a guide, not a script.

The key is to think fast and act iteratively: start with simple ideas for posts, conversation points, or small events you can test right away.

Don't plan too far ahead. Plan just enough to experiment, observe, learn, and adjust. The more responsive you are to how people react and what they care about, the more authentic and impactful your campaign will become.
