📝 Level 1 · Why Energy Now

Climate & Energy Transition Glossary

Buzzwords decoded — how to explain the complex stuff to a kid (or your confused uncle). Every term comes with a textbook definition and a human one.

Showing 20 of 20 terms

1
1.5°C Target ›
📖 Textbook

The goal to limit global temperature rise to 1.5 degrees Celsius above pre-industrial levels to avoid catastrophe.

"The Safety Line"
🧒 For an 8-year-old

If your body temperature goes up 1.5 degrees, you feel sick. If it goes higher, you go to the hospital. We need to keep Earth out of the hospital.

A
Adaptation ›
📖 Textbook

Adjusting to current or expected climate change and its effects.

"Buying an Umbrella"
🧒 For an 8-year-old

The rain is already here, so we need to learn how to stay dry — like building higher walls against floods.

C
Carbon Footprint ›
📖 Textbook

The total amount of greenhouse gases generated by our actions.

"Your Invisible Shadow"
🧒 For an 8-year-old

Every time you drive a car or buy plastic, you leave a mark on the planet. We want to make that mark smaller.

C
Carbon Offsetting ›
📖 Textbook

Compensating for emissions by funding an equivalent carbon dioxide saving elsewhere.

"Paying for Chores"
🧒 For an 8-year-old

You made a mess in your room, but instead of cleaning it, you pay your brother to clean his room. (It helps, but your room is still messy!)

C
Circular Economy ›
📖 Textbook

A model of production and consumption involving reusing, repairing, and recycling materials as long as possible.

"Legos"
🧒 For an 8-year-old

When you finish building a Lego castle, you don't throw the bricks in the trash. You take them apart and build a spaceship. No waste!

C
Climate Change ›
📖 Textbook

Long-term shifts in temperatures and weather patterns.

"The Earth is Getting a Fever"
🧒 For an 8-year-old

The weather is acting weird — storms are scarier and summers are hotter — because the planet is sick.

C
Climate Justice ›
📖 Textbook

Framing global warming as an ethical and political issue, acknowledging that those who did the least to cause it suffer the most.

"Fair Play"
🧒 For an 8-year-old

The rich kids made the mess, but the poor kids are being forced to clean it up. Climate Justice means making sure the rules are fair for everyone.

D
Decarbonisation ›
📖 Textbook

The reduction or removal of carbon dioxide emissions from an economy or sector.

"Going on a Diet"
🧒 For an 8-year-old

We are cutting out the junk food (carbon) from our daily lives to get healthy.

E
Energy Efficiency ›
📖 Textbook

Using less energy to perform the same task.

"Sipping Slowly"
🧒 For an 8-year-old

Getting the same amount of fun from your juice box, but making it last longer so you don't waste it.

E
ESG (Environmental, Social & Governance) ›
📖 Textbook

A set of standards measuring a business's impact on society, the environment, and how transparent it is.

"A Company Report Card"
🧒 For an 8-year-old

It's not just about how much money a company makes, but if they are being nice to people and the planet.

F
Fossil Fuels ›
📖 Textbook

Non-renewable energy sources formed from ancient remains of plants and animals (Coal, Oil, Gas).

"Dinosaur Juice"
🧒 For an 8-year-old

It's old stuff we dig up and burn. It gives us power, but the smoke makes the Earth sick.

G
Global Boiling ›
📖 Textbook

A term coined by the UN to emphasize that "Global Warming" is no longer a strong enough description.

"It's Not Just Warm — It's Cooking"
🧒 For an 8-year-old

Imagine leaving a pot of soup on the stove too long. That's us right now.

G
Greenhouse Gases (GHG) ›
📖 Textbook

Gases (like CO₂ & Methane) that trap heat in the atmosphere.

"A Heavy Blanket"
🧒 For an 8-year-old

The more gas we pump out, the thicker the blanket gets, making the Earth sweat.

G
Greenwashing ›
📖 Textbook

Deceptive marketing used to persuade the public that an organization's products, aims, and policies are environmentally friendly.

"Faking It"
🧒 For an 8-year-old

It's like eating a cookie and calling it a salad just because the wrapper is green. Don't be fooled!

J
Just Transition ›
📖 Textbook

Greening the economy in a way that is fair and inclusive to all workers and communities.

"Moving House Together"
🧒 For an 8-year-old

We are moving to a cleaner house (Green Energy), but we can't leave our friends (coal workers) behind. We help them pack and move too.

L
Loss and Damage ›
📖 Textbook

Impacts of climate change that cannot be avoided or adapted to (like land lost to rising seas).

"You Break It, You Buy It"
🧒 For an 8-year-old

Big countries broke the climate. Now our islands are sinking. They should pay for the damage.

M
Mitigation ›
📖 Textbook

Actions taken to reduce or prevent the emission of greenhouse gases.

"Stop the Leak"
🧒 For an 8-year-old

Fixing the hole in the roof so the rain stops coming in.

N
Net Zero ›
📖 Textbook

Achieving a balance between the greenhouse gases put into the atmosphere and those taken out.

"The Bathtub Rule"
🧒 For an 8-year-old

If you can't turn off the tap (pollution) completely yet, you must open the drain (planting trees / tech) so the water doesn't overflow.

R
Renewable Energy ›
📖 Textbook

Energy from sources that naturally replenish themselves (Sun, Wind, Water).

"Infinite Magic Power"
🧒 For an 8-year-old

Unlike coal, the sun and wind never run out. We can use them forever without making smoke.

T
Tipping Point ›
📖 Textbook

A critical threshold that, when crossed, leads to large and often irreversible changes in the climate system.

"The Jenga Tower"
🧒 For an 8-year-old

You can pull out a few blocks and it wobbles. But if you pull out one wrong block… the whole thing crashes down and you can't put it back.
